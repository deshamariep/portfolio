Report

Pain Points

#1 Price:

The price does not match the value given, size and quality are not worth the price being paid by customers.

  • Drinks come in small sizes and you have to pay to add toppings or change to a bigger size
  • Competitors have drinks in larger sizes and better quality

Support:

60% of our findings show that customers are concerned about the price of the drinks and the value they are willing to pay. The customers complain that UME’s drinks are too expensive for their worth compared to other places such as iTea or T%.

#2 Promotion:

Minimal outreach to non-followers and non-current customers.

  • Some participants have stated that they have never seen any promotions from UME
    • No promotions = no new customers / retaining current ones

Support:

80% of the customers said they have never seen a promotion from UME (social media, ads, flyers, etc.). Most customers said they only knew of UME from word of mouth or from unexpectedly coming across the store.

#3 Product:

Drink quality is worse compared to some other competitors.

  • UME’s menu is limited
    • Especially with their milk tea selection, which is one of the more popular styles amongst boba drinkers

Support:

40% of our findings show that customers are not satisfied with the drink quality they get for the price that they pay for the drink. Customers are complaining that the drinks are very average and taste watered down. There is nothing that stands out about UME’s drinks.

#4 Place:

Small areas for gathering within the store front, and a slightly aloof site.

  • Customers are not able to sit down for long or find seating when visiting
  • Location not easily accessible, the store is far from campus and downtown

Support:

From our customer survey, 80% of the customers said they enjoy the aesthetics of the store, as well as the surprise plushies that come with certain drinks. These two aspects were the main reasons on why customers enjoy the store so much and why they would go back.

Marketing Challenges

  • Ume’s aesthetics out-performs its competitors
  • Quality is slightly below others
  • Value is below its competitors
  • Menu Variety is a little below others
  • Customer Service is slightly above others

Marketing weaknesses based on the Buyer’s Journey

#1 Awareness:

  • Too many options available for buyer to choose from (nearby competitors: iTea, T%, Raretea, Sharetea, etc.)
  • All boba shops have relatively similar drink menus
    • Question to ask: Which boba shop provides the most value without having to spend too much money?

#2 Consideration:

  • All shops mostly offer same variety
  • Nothing really stands out at UME
    • Question to ask: What makes UME different from other competitors?

#3 Purchase:

  • Feeling satisfied when spending hard earned money at UME
    • Question to ask: Are there any sales or promotion that motivate me to buy from UME?

#4 Retention:

  • Making sure UME is always reliable and consistent with their quality (fast customer service, staff listens to customers’ needs, drinks are delicious, etc.)
    • Question to ask: Do I feel satisfied every time I spend my hard earned money at UME?

#5 Loyalty:

  • Want a go-to boba shop that can make my experience perfect every time
    • Question to ask: Do I get an incentive/reward if I continue to spend my money at UME?

Customer Segmentation

We chose to implement customer segmentation in our marketing strategy to better understand and connect with our diverse customer base. By dividing our market into distinct segments based on demographics, behaviors, and preferences, we gained invaluable insights into what drives each group's purchasing decisions. This approach allowed us to tailor our marketing campaigns more precisely, delivering messages and offers that resonate deeply with each segment.
