TAP Series

Role: Executive Assistant

Focus: User Interface Design

Duration: 9 months

Tools: Figma, Canva, Notion

Read Time: 5-7 Minutes

Optimizing Usability & Production Systems

By addressing user pain points and system inefficiencies, we created targeted solutions to enhance the overall user experience, streamline workflows, and ensure better system integrity.

TAP Series is an industry-leading provider of 100% online, ANSI-accredited compliance training, specializing in courses like Food Safety Manager Certification that serve employers nationwide. Drawing on detailed user feedback, I identified key areas where the platform’s usability and system processes could be improved. I developed targeted UI solutions, then collaborated with the development team to implement them across core workflows.

1. Improving Post-Exam Clarity & User Trust

Problem: Users completing the Food Safety Manager training were left uncertain about what to do next. The messaging after the practice exam was vague, especially for those who failed, and many users under our partnered Retail Grocery Stores’ encountered login issues when transitioning to the affiliated ANSI proctoring site. These gaps led to confusion, delayed certification, and an increase in customer support calls.

The goal was to improve post-exam clarity, guide users based on pass/fail outcomes, and proactively reduce common technical issues during the exam handoff process. Additionally, to create a client-specific version for Albertson’s that aligned with their training standards.

I rewrote the post-exam UI messaging, designed new guidance components, and collaborated with stakeholders to ensure alignment with training compliance and feasible implementation.

Solution:

  • Designed outcome-specific messages: separate UI messaging flows for users who passed and those who failed, each with tailored next steps.
  • Added an instructional screen before the ANSI link was shown, helping users avoid common login issues (e.g. email access, forgotten credentials).
  • Created a custom version for corporate clients (Albertson’s/Safeway) that met their internal compliance needs.
  • Ensured the design maintained visual clarity, used friendly tone and hierarchy, and stayed consistent with the rest of the TAP system UI.

2. Designing Secure Admin Workflows

Problem: After enrolling students, admins occasionally realized that names had been misspelled, entered incorrectly, or did not match legal documentation. However, allowing unrestricted editing of student names post-enrollment posed a risk — some users were modifying names to falsely represent training completion, potentially bypassing required certification steps.

The goal here was to design a secure yet flexible system that allows for genuine name corrections while preventing misuse that could compromise the integrity of training records and certifications.

I was responsible for designing a solution that balanced administrative flexibility with system integrity. This involved understanding the misuse scenarios, gathering input from support staff, and creating a user-friendly flow that aligned with compliance standards.

Solution: A guided correction flow that removed direct editing access from the admin interface after enrollment. I designed a pop-up that appeared when an admin attempted to edit a name, providing clear instructions on how to change a name through proper channels. This ensured that errors could be corrected while preventing unauthorized or fraudulent modifications.

3. AI-Assisted Email Campaigns & Learning Content

Problem: The marketing team and Managing Member needed a faster way to scale email campaigns and generate learning materials across multiple courses. Existing processes used design focused to HTML softwares, drafting study content was time-consuming and inconsistent, limiting the speed of outreach and content development.

The goal was to leverage design systems and AI tools to streamline content creation for email campaigns and learning materials, enabling the team to scale outreach and documentation without sacrificing quality or brand consistency.

I used ChatGPT’s project capabilities to generate structured drafts for both email templates and course study guides/syllabi. I developed HTML guidelines, then applied our internal design and brand standards to refine these drafts for clarity, usability, and visual consistency. I also set up a system for the marketing team to easily utilize the templates across their weekly email campaigns.

Solution:

  • Leveraged ChatGPT to draft HTML email content and educational documents across multiple courses
  • Integrated brand-aligned layouts and tone standards into AI-generated templates to ensure a cohesive, user-friendly experience
  • Developed a repeatable workflow the marketing team used to send over 50,000 emails per week using these scalable templates.

This approach dramatically reduced the time required for content generation, allowing teams to scale outreach and documentation with consistent quality. The AI-supported workflow became a sustainable solution for high-volume campaign needs, improving speed without compromising design integrity.

Conclusion:

Each of these projects contributed to optimizing usability and design by streamlining workflows, enhancing user guidance, and ensuring system integrity. The key achievements included improved user experience, faster issue resolution, automation of administrative tasks, and clear communication of changes or guidance for administrators and end-users.
