Optimizing Usability & Production Systems

By addressing user pain points and system inefficiencies, we created targeted solutions to enhance the overall user experience, streamline workflows, and ensure better system integrity.


TAP Series is an industry-leading provider of 100% online, ANSI-accredited compliance training, specializing in courses like Food Safety Manager Certification that serve employers nationwide. Drawing on detailed user feedback, I identified key areas where the platform’s usability and system processes could be improved. I developed targeted UI solutions, then collaborated with the development team to implement them across core workflows.

1. Improving Post-Practice Exam Clarity & User Trust


Heuristic Design Evaluation

UX Writing

Context


Users completing the Food Safety Manager training faced unclear next steps after practice exams and frequent login issues when accessing the ANSI proctoring site. This confusion led to certification delays and increased support calls.


The goal was to improve post-exam clarity, guide users based on pass/fail outcomes, and proactively reduce common technical issues during the exam handoff process. Additionally, to create a client-specific version for Albertson’s that aligned with their training standards.

Approach


Preliminary Research

I began with a heuristic evaluation to quickly identify design issues that could harm user experience. I found the following discrepancies from Jakob Nielsen’s 10 Usability Heuristics:


Visibility of System Status

The success messages and score outcomes where out of the way and confusing. Users didn’t know whether they passed or failed immediately.


Recognition Rather Than Recall

There was no provided guidance increasing the need for users to phone in for support on what to do next. Actions were not visible at the right time.


Aesthetic and Minimalist Design

Support information was added to the success message causing clutter, preventing the main outcome stand out.


Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors

For failing users, there was no immediate recovery path, leading to confusion or dead ends.


Updated Separate Messaging Flows


for fail/pass outcomes

Both updated fail and pass screens use visual hierarchy to guide user attention.


Success messages and score outcomes draw the eye, while bolded guidance text and strategically placed links provide clear next steps.


Support information is separated to reduce cognitive load and clutter, with passing users directed to ANSI registration and failing users guided to retake the practice test.



Error Prevention & Next Steps


Instructional Page

I then added pre-ANSI instructional screen for passing users to prevent common login issues. Taking note of Albertson's/Safeway compliance requirements while maintaining visual consistency with the TAP system.


This screen reinforces user confidence by combining clear status visibility, strong visual hierarchy, and actionable next steps, pairing success confirmation with bolded key details, color cues, and direct pathways to reduce errors and guide users seamlessly forward.


Solution

I redesigned training flow screens to reduce confusion during exam transitions. The updated messaging clarified login steps, decreased support requests, and ensured smoother certification progress. I also created a custom version meeting Retail Grocery Stores' compliance requirements.

2. Designing Secure Admin Workflows


User Journey

Interaction Design

Context


After enrollment, admins found student/employee names were often misspelled or didn't match legal documentation. However, unrestricted name editing posed risks, users could falsify training completion and bypass certification requirements.


The goal here was to design a secure yet flexible system that allows for genuine name corrections while preventing misuse that could compromise the integrity of training records and certifications.

Approach


A Heuristic Evaluation

I began with a heuristic evaluation


Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors

Previously, admins had no ability to edit names, but the system also lacked clear guidance to help them understand what could and couldn’t be changed.


Visibility of System Status

No feedback when an edit attempt happens, so admins don’t know why the action is restricted or what they should do next.

Admin Editing Guide

A pop-up that identifies unrestricted name editing after enrollment and highlighted the appropriate fields with icons. When admins attempted to make changes, the system surfaced, instuctions to a dedicated page with step-by-step guidance, making the process intuitive, secure, and easy to follow.

Solution

A secure correction system that guided admins through proper name change procedures while protecting certification records. Balancing administrative needs with security requirements, ensuring accurate student data without enabling fraudulent modifications.

3. AI-Assisted Email Campaigns & Learning Content


Consistency & Brand Cohesion

AI-Assisted Design

Design Systems Documentation

Context


The marketing team needed to scale email campaigns and generate learning materials more efficiently. Current design-focused to HTML processes were slow and inconsistent, creating bottlenecks in outreach and content development across multiple courses.


The goal was to leverage design systems and AI tools to automate content creation for email campaigns and learning materials, enabling the team to scale outreach and documentation without sacrificing quality or brand consistency.

Approach


Comparative Analysis

I began with an analysis of existing approaches, looking at both internal workflows and external tools.


Prior Internal Solutions

The marketing team was using BeeFree to design each email individually for every course campaign. While this allowed for creative flexibility, it was also extremely time-intensive and slowed campaign production.


Competitors and Analogous Products

I reviewed other tools that convert designs into HTML emails, including template builders and emerging AI-powered products, to benchmark alternative approaches and identify opportunities for efficiency.

AI Powered Design


ChatGPT Projects

HTML & CSS

I developed instructions, with HTML and CSS elements, for the AI to use as the email framework. By incorporating our brand standards and design guidelines, I refined these outputs for clarity and visual consistency. I then leveraged ChatGPT's project capabilities to generate structured HTML email templates and course study guides. This created an easily accessible system for the marketing team to streamline their weekly email campaigns.

Solution

This AI-supported workflow dramatically reduced content generation time while maintaining consistent quality across campaigns. Leveraged ChatGPT to draft HTML email content and educational documents, integrating brand-aligned layouts to ensure cohesive experiences. The repeatable system enabled the marketing team to send over 50,000 emails per week using scalable templates.

Conclusion

Each of these projects contributed to optimizing usability and design by streamlining workflows, enhancing user guidance, and ensuring system integrity. The key achievements included improved user experience, faster issue resolution, automation of administrative tasks, and clear communication of changes or guidance from developers to end-users.
