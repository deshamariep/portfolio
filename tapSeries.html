1. Improving Post-Practice Exam Clarity & User Trust

UX Writing





Context

The Problem

Logistics

Users completing the Food Safety Manager training faced unclear next steps after practice exams and frequent login issues when accessing the ANSI proctoring site. This confusion led to certification delays and increased support calls.

The goal was to improve post-exam clarity, guide users based on pass/fail outcomes, and proactively reduce common technical issues during the exam handoff process. Additionally, to create a client-specific version for retail employeres that aligned with their training standards.

──────────────────

Duration: 3 Days

Stakeholders: Managing Member, Users - Students and Employees, and Development Team

Heuristic Evaluation

Usability Research

I began with this approach to quickly identify design issues that could harm user experience. I found the following discrepancies from Jakob Nielsen’s 10 Usability Heuristics:

Visibility of System Status

The success messages and score outcomes were out of the way and confusing. Users didn’t know whether they passed or failed immediately.

Recognition Rather Than Recall

There was no provided guidance increasing the need for users to phone in for support on what to do next. Actions were not visible at the right time.

Aesthetic and Minimalist Design

Support information was combined to the success message causing clutter, preventing the main outcome stand out.

Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors

For failing users, there was no immediate recovery path, leading to confusion or dead ends.


Clearer Messaging Flows

Fail/Pass Outcomes

I updated both fail and pass screens using a clarifying visual hierarchy to guide user attention.

Underlined failed messages or punctuated success messages for score outcomes draw the eye.

Provided clear next steps with bolded guidance text and strategically placed links encouraging failing users to retake the practice test and passing users to ANSI Exam registration.

Support information is separated to reduce cognitive load.

Fail



Pass

Error Prevention & Next Steps

Instructional Page

I then added a pre-ANSI instructional screen for passing users to prevent common login issues. Taking note of compliance requirements while maintaining visual consistency with the TAP system.

This screen reinforces user confidence by combining clear status visibility, strong visual hierarchy, and actionable next steps, pairing success confirmation with bolded key details, color cues, and direct pathways to reduce errors and guide users seamlessly forward.


Streamlining Exam Transitions for a Smoother Training Experience

Solution

I redesigned training flow screens to reduce confusion during exam transitions. The updated messaging clarified login steps, decreased support requests, and ensured smoother certification progress.

2. Designing Secure Admin Workflows

Usability

Interaction Design





Context

The Problem

Logistics

After enrollment, admins found student/employee names were often misspelled or didn't match legal documentation. However, unrestricted name editing posed risks, users could falsify training completion and bypass certification requirements.

The goal here was to design a secure yet flexible system that allows for genuine name corrections while preventing misuse that could compromise the integrity of training records and certifications.

──────────────────

Duration: 3 Days

Stakeholders: Managing Member, Users: Teachers and Admins, and Development Team

Understanding the Workflow

Usability Observation

Heuristic Evaluation

While assisting admins over the phone, I often guided them through adding students in the system. During these calls, I began to notice a pattern, whenever a student’s name was entered incorrectly, their tasks were frequently interrupted. Because only I or another team member could manually update names, we had to double-check that the student’s information was accurate and confirm they had not already started or completed training. These repeated moments provided me with firsthand insight into how confusing and disruptive the process felt for administrators, sparking the idea for a more intuitive and secure name correction flow.


After an evaluation I discovered these holes in the Usability Heuristics:

Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors

Admins have no ability to edit names, but the system also lacked clear guidance to help them understand what could and couldn’t be changed.

Visibility of System Status

No feedback when an edit attempt happens, so admins don’t know why the action is restricted or what they should do next.

Admin Editing Guide

A pop-up that identifies restricted name changes after enrollment and highlighted the editable fields with icons. When admins attempted to make changes, the system surfaces, instuctions to a dedicated pages on their dashboard pannel with step-by-step guidance, making the process intuitive, secure, and easy to follow.


Streamlining Admin Changes While Protecting Certification Integrity

Solution

A secure correction system that guided admins through proper name change procedures while protecting certification records. Balancing administrative needs with security requirements, ensuring accurate student data without enabling fraudulent modifications.

3. AI-Assisted Email Campaigns & Learning Content

Marketing & Brand Identity

AI-Assisted Design

Design Systems





Context

The Problem

Logistics

The marketing team needed to scale email campaigns and generate learning materials more efficiently. Current design-focused to HTML processes were slow and inconsistent, creating bottlenecks in outreach and content development across multiple courses.

The goal was to leverage design systems and AI tools to automate content creation for email campaigns and learning materials, enabling the team to scale outreach and documentation without sacrificing quality or brand consistency.

──────────────────

Duration: 1 Week

Stakeholders: Managing Member, Opperations Manager, Development Team, and Marketing Team

Comparative Analysis

Systematic Comparison

Analyzing Product Features

I began with an analysis of existing approaches, looking at both internal workflows and external tools.

Prior Internal Solutions

The marketing team was using BeeFree to design each email and export as an HTML file individually for every course campaign. While this allowed for creative flexibility, it was also extremely time-intensive and slowed campaign production.

Competitors and Analogous Products

I reviewed other tools that convert designs into HTML emails, including template builders and emerging AI-powered products, to benchmark alternative approaches and identify opportunities for efficiency.

I found a system using AI. It could be given content and instructions on a standard design to create emails, that could then be exported as an HTML file in under 3 minutes. If needed the Marketing Team's email designer could also add addtional instructions to make the email more catered to specific courses.

AI Powered Design

ChatGPT Projects

HTML & CSS

I developed instructions using HTML and CSS elements, for the AI to use as the email framework. Incorporating our brand standards and design guidelines, I refined these outputs for clarity and visual consistency. I then leveraged ChatGPT's project capabilities to generate structured HTML email templates and course study guides. This created an easily accessible system for the marketing team to streamline their weekly email campaigns.

Scaling Content Creation Through AI-Assisted Design

Solution

This AI-supported workflow dramatically reduced content generation time while maintaining consistent quality across campaigns. Leveraged ChatGPT to draft HTML email content and educational documents, integrating brand-aligned layouts to ensure cohesive experiences. The repeatable system enabled the marketing team to send over 50,000 emails per week using scalable templates.

Conclusion

Each of these projects contributed to optimizing usability and design by streamlining workflows, enhancing user guidance, and ensuring system integrity. The key achievements included improved user experience, faster issue resolution, automation of administrative tasks, and clear communication of changes or guidance from developers to end-users.



Takeaways

Throughout these projects, I collaborated closely with stakeholders across departments to ensure clarity, alignment, and feasibility at every stage of the design process. Working with the agile method taught me how to adapt quickly, communicate progress effectively, and balance technical constraints with user needs. I designed for both consumer-facing experiences and internal administrative tools, gaining a deeper understanding of how cohesive design systems can support both sides of a SaaS-based e-learning platform. These experiences strengthened my ability to translate complex requirements into clear, intuitive, and scalable design solutions.
